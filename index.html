Building the Intelligence for Embodied AI

As a Senior Researcher at the Robotics Lab at Plaksha University, I operate at the intersection of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) and Embodied AI. My mission is to bridge the gap between abstract reasoning and physical interaction by developing foundational world models that possess deep spatial and temporal awareness.

My research is driven by a fundamental question: How can we empower robots with the same intuitive understanding of the physical world that humans possess? To answer this, I focus on learning latent representations and enhancing reward modeling using LLMs and VLMs. I believe that increasing the spatial intelligence of these models is the critical unlock for the next generation of general-purpose robotic systems.

With an M.S. from IIT Madras and a strong background in image processing and computer vision, I bring a specialized perspective to the frontier of Post-AGI science, focusing on how machines perceive and reason about their environment.

Research Projects

Current Research Focus

Robust Terrain Adaptation for Quadrupeds

Scaling UC Berkeley's Robust Motor Adaptation (RMA) to enable agile locomotion across diverse and challenging terrains via proprioceptive feedback.

VLA for Semantic Navigation

Developing Vision-Language-Action policies using VLMs as high-level planners to bridge long-horizon reasoning with low-level robotic control.

Causal JEPA for World Models

Building over Joint-Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA) to incorporate causal constraints, enhancing the spatial intelligence of world models.

Reward Shaping via LLMs/VLMs

Leveraging foundation models to automate complex reward modeling in Reinforcement Learning, accelerating skill acquisition in embodied agents.
